Problem Statement

In Uganda, boda boda riders constitute a large and growing segment of road users. They are essential to daily transport for millions, especially in densely populated urban centers like Kampala. However, they are also one of the most vulnerable groups on the road.

Critical Issues

Road accidents involving boda bodas occur frequently, with many resulting in serious injury or death. A critical problem is that when these accidents occur, especially in remote areas or late at night, there may be no one around to help. Injured riders may be unconscious or physically unable to call for assistance, leading to delayed medical intervention and avoidable loss of life.

Additionally, many existing safety efforts do not address the lack of real-time incident detection and response. Most boda boda riders cannot afford smart helmets or emergency alert devices that are available in other countries, creating a technological gap in local road safety infrastructure.

This project seeks to close that gap with a low-cost, Arduino-powered solution that enhances rider safety without compromising helmet integrity or comfort.

Proposed Solution

Smart Detection System

The Smart Boda Helmet system is designed as a practical and affordable embedded solution to improve rider safety through automated crash detection and emergency communication.

Built around the Arduino platform, this system uses a gyroscope and accelerometer module (MPU-6050) to continuously monitor the rider's head movement and orientation. If a sudden impact or abnormal tilt is detected, the system begins a countdown to check for inactivity.

If the rider does not move within the defined window (e.g., 10â€“15 seconds), the system automatically triggers an alert through a GSM module (SIM800L). The alert is sent via SMS to a preset emergency contact, containing a custom message such as 'Rider down. Immediate help needed.' With the addition of a GPS module, future versions could include exact coordinates in the SMS.

To ensure flexibility, the helmet features a single-button user interface: a short press activates the emergency alert manually (for conscious riders in distress), while a double press cancels an impending alert (useful in the event of false triggers or minor incidents).

Long-term Vision: This helmet could be developed into a marketable product or distributed through partnerships with NGOs, SafeBoda, or government safety programs to help reduce motorcycle-related fatalities across Uganda.

Key Features

Automatic Crash Detection

MPU-6050 sensor continuously monitors head movement and detects sudden impacts or abnormal tilts automatically.

SMS Emergency Alerts

SIM800L GSM module sends instant SMS alerts to preset emergency contacts when an incident is detected.

Manual Emergency Button

Single-button interface allows riders to manually trigger alerts or cancel false alarms with simple press patterns.

Rechargeable Battery

Long-lasting rechargeable battery system ensures the device remains operational throughout the day.

Non-Invasive Design

Components integrated within helmet padding without compromising structural safety or user comfort.

Affordable Solution

Low-cost Arduino-based design makes advanced safety technology accessible to all boda boda riders.
